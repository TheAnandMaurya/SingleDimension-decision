🧠 Multi-Dimensional Decision Analyzer

P × D × C × K Analysis with Benefit-Cost Calculation

Step 1: Add Choices (C)

Enter all the choices that people need to decide between.
Example: Study for Exam, Watch Reels, Go to Gym

Your Choices

Step 2: Add Persons (P)

Add all the people you want to analyze.
Example: Person 1, Person 2, John, Sarah

Your Persons

Step 3: Add Dates/Times (D)

Add all the time periods for analysis.
Example: Date 1, Date 2, Morning, Evening, January 2026

Your Dates

Add Criteria (K)

Define the criteria that influence decisions.
Benefit: Higher is better (e.g., Skill Growth, Enjoyment)
Cost: Higher is worse (e.g., Time Required, Stress)

Your Criteria

Criteria Weights: Weight[p][d][k]
For each Person × Date combination, assign importance to each criteria.
Total inputs: P × D × K = 0
Choice Ratings: Rating[p][d][c][k]
For each Person × Date × Choice combination, rate each criteria.
Total inputs: P × D × C × K = 0

🏆 Decision Results

Formula:
Benefit = Σ (Weight[p][d][k] × Rating[p][d][c][k]) for Benefit criteria
Cost = Σ (Weight[p][d][k] × Rating[p][d][c][k]²) for Cost criteria
Final Score = Benefit - Cost
